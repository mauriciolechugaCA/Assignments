Biography

Leonardo da Vinci, born in Vinci, Italy, in 1452, was a true Renaissance man whose life was characterized by an insatiable curiosity and remarkable talents. He excelled not only as a painter but also as a scientist, engineer, inventor, and polymath. Da Vinci's artistic legacy includes iconic works like the "Mona Lisa" and "The Last Supper"," which continue to captivate audiences worldwide. His notebooks are a testament to his diverse interests, containing detailed anatomical studies, inventions, and scientific observations ahead of his time. Da Vinci's legacy transcends generations, making him one of history's most revered and influential figures, embodying the spirit of creativity and intellectual exploration of the Renaissance era.

DaVinci's Work
Title Year Medium Description
Mona Lisa (La Gioconda) 1503-1506 Oil on panel Iconic portrait known for the subject's enigmatic smile and detailed landscape background.
The Last Supper (Il Cenacolo) 1495-1498 Fresco Depicts the moment Jesus announces betrayal, capturing the reactions of his disciples.
Vitruvian Man c. 1490 Pen and ink with wash over metalpoint on paper Illustration exploring the proportions of the human body within a square and a circle.
Annunciation c. 1472-1475 Oil on wood Depicts the angel Gabriel announcing to the Virgin Mary that she will conceive the Son of God.
Lady with an Ermine c. 1489-1490 Oil on wood Portrait of Cecilia Gallerani, holding an ermine, showcasing da Vinci's mastery of capturing expression.
